An Analysis of Global Human Development

Development

In order to explore human development and their aspects, we need to identify its definition within the scope of our exploration and what consititutes human development. The diagram presented on the right provides a breakdown of the term Development by displaying parts that make up its whole.

Human Development Index

Human Development Index (HDI) is a normalised measure of a nation's development. These values are obtained by using a function which considers factors including health, education and economic factors such as Gross Domestic Product (GDP) and Gross National Income (GNI). The map shown displays the HDI values for each country. This statistic can provide valuable insights into the level of development, allowing for policies or funding to be provided to developing or less developed nations.

These values display the United Nations Development Program's thresholds for development, which are classified using the 3 step colour legend. The colour scale represents first world countries with a darker colour and third world countries with a lighter shade
